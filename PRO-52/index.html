Interesting Facts about Camels!
  • Camels can reach 7 feet in height (at the hump) and weigh up to 1500 pounds.
  • Their eyes have three eyelids and two rows of eyelashes that prevent sand to enter their eyes.
  • Don’t make a camel angry – they can spit as a way to distract whatever they think is a threat.

Interesting Facts about Chameleons!
  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  • Changing skin color is an imaportant part of communication among chameleons.
  • Most chameleons have a prehostile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Interesting Facts about Cicada!
  • They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus. (Females make a much quieter sound by flicking their wings.) Cicadas don’t sing at night, so don’t blame them if you can’t sleep.

Interesting Facts about Bears!
  • Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals. They have far superior navigation skills to humans; excellent memories; large brain to body ratio; and use tools in various contexts from play to hunting.
  • Bears care deeply about family members. They will risk their lives and even fight to the death in order to save a cub or sibling from danger.
  • Bears grieve deeply for others. Cubs are known to moan and cry when separated from their mothers. This can go on for weeks if their mothers are killed by hunters.
